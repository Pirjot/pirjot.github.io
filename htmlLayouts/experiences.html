Experiences

The National STEM Honor Society (NSTEM)

Media Director and Web Intern

The National STEM Honor Society is a startup in Massachusetts that looks to open chapters in schools nationwide at any level to promote the learning of STEM in communities that don't have the resources to fund STEM education.

I joined NSTEM during my first year at community college (2020-2021) and soon climbed the ranks to Director of the Media Department. As Director, I worked with a team of diverse interns in planning and executing the creation of different forms of media for both internal and external use by the company. My biggest accomplishment as Director was starting the "Intern Experience Campaign," an idea I had to help the receival of the company's internship program by potential students.

On the Web side, I helped create site content through Wordpress for the company's website, as well as developed a Google API script to help display the various chapters the company had opened nationwide to clients. My biggest accomplishment on Web was the Internal Intern Portal, another idea I had to help assist Directors and NSTEM's departments on the management of intern hours and tasks, which I had cooperated with some other directors on to put systems in place.

Ohlone ASOC's R&D Branch

Student Coordinator

During my second year at community college (2021-2022), I joined the Tech Student Government Branch of Ohlone's ASOC, R&D. As a Student Coordinator, I was assigned mainly to assist the Hackathon team in planning for Ohlone Hackathon 3.0, an in-person event to showcase the abilities of Ohlone's STEM community.

I helped planned different necessities for the event, such as planning merch, food, security, and rooming. During the event I led my own workshop where I presented for my club Dot-Slash CS (see below!) and I had a blast meeting a lot of fellow STEM students at the college.

Ohlone's Dot-Slash Computer Science

President

I was a member of Dot-Slash Computer Science for two years and served as a president for my second year. A majority of my time at Dot-Slash was dedicated to the development of my brainchild, MakeMyFuture. MakeMyFuture was an idea I had in response to the multiple students I had met during my time at Ohlone who had trouble with deciding the most efficient schedule plan that they should undertake to optimize their chances at transferring to their dream colleges. There were some more unfortunate cases of students being completely unaware of necessary requirements for the majors they were following; those cases ended with the students having to take extra years of courses, a decision which could have been completely prevented.

I talk more about MakeMyFuture in the Projects section, its development, and where you can find the site. MakeMyFuture is definitely the first "official" project that I was proud to have finished, and its something that motivates me to develop more solutions to problems that my peers and I encounter in the world.
